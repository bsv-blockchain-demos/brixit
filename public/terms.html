3. Wallet-based authentication — important disclosure

BRIX uses a cryptographic wallet system for identity and authentication rather than email addresses and passwords. By using BRIX you acknowledge and agree to the following:

What is a wallet?

A wallet is software (such as the Mycelia app) that generates and stores a cryptographic key pair on your device. The private key never leaves your device. BRIX stores only the corresponding public key (your "identity key") to identify your account.

What is the BSV blockchain standard?

BRIX's authentication system is based on the BSV (Bitcoin SV) blockchain cryptographic identity standard. This is an open standard for public-key cryptography. BRIX does not write your personal data to the blockchain; however, the identity key format is derived from this standard and your public key may appear in records associated with any on-chain interactions you independently make using the same wallet.

What is a certificate?

When you create a BRIX account, we issue a cryptographic membership certificate into your wallet. This certificate confirms your membership status. It is stored on your device (not our servers) and is used by the BRIX app to confirm your identity at login. You can revoke it by removing it from your wallet or requesting account deletion.

  • You are solely responsible for maintaining access to your wallet and private key.
  • BRIX cannot recover your account if you lose access to your wallet. There is no password reset.
  • If your wallet is compromised, you should contact us immediately to revoke your certificate.
  • Your identity key is a pseudonymous public identifier. It does not inherently reveal your real name unless you choose to provide a display name.
